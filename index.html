Student Intern at the Gaming Research for Integration and Learning labratory. Worked with Wright State University's Calamityville site, and used Unreal Engine 4 and the Distributed Interactive Simulation(DIS) networking protocol to create a mixed-reality multiplayer training simulation to help with Rocket Propelled Grenade(RPG) training. Mixed-reality simulations allows for real world integration of models such that if it is moved in reality, it will appear as being moved in the simulation. This was done by having multiple trackers on a model RPG as well as having sensors for GPS location, orientation, velocity, acceleration, and many more pieces of data. This research Decreased the cost of RPG training by over 85% and created a more feasible alternativethat is safer, cheaper, allows for more scenarios, and overall a better training experience.
